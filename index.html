An early summer honeymoon, perfectly timed.

This itinerary departs Tuesday, July 6 — just two days after your July 3rd wedding — for 21 unhurried days through northern and central Italy.

⌖

Florence, Curated

Limited to essentials only: Duomo, Uffizi, Ponte Vecchio, Accademia (David), and Santa Croce.

⚓

Islands & Coasts

Ravenna mosaics, San Marino towers, and overnight on Elba (Napoleon's villas).

✦

Historic Stops

Bologna's university, cross the Rubicon at Savignano sul Rubicone, overnight in San Marino, day-stop in Assisi.

⊕

Rome & South

Five full days in Rome, then two nights in Naples (with a Capri day trip), followed by Pompeii and three relaxing nights in Praiano on the Amalfi Coast.

✝

Pilgrimage

Your route follows Italy's great pilgrim roads.

  • Venice: St Mark's Basilica with the relics of the Evangelist; Torcello's Santa Maria Assunta mosaics.
  • Ravenna: San Vitale, Galla Placidia – early Christian mosaics.
  • Bologna: Basilica of San Domenico – tomb of St Dominic, founder of the Order of Preachers.
  • Assisi: Basilica of St Francis – Upper and Lower churches, tomb of Il Poverello.
  • Rome: The four major papal basilicas – St Mary Major, St Peter's (Sunday Angelus), St John Lateran with the Scala Santa, St Paul Outside the Walls. Plus San Clemente and the Catacombs of San Callisto.
  • Amalfi: Duomo di Sant'Andrea – crypt holding relics of St Andrew the Apostle.
⌛

History

From empire to republic to exile.

  • Roman foundations: Cross the Rubicon at Savignano, walk Pompeii's streets, Rome's Colosseum, Forum, Palatine, Pantheon, and Appian Way.
  • Byzantine Ravenna: San Vitale's Justinian and Theodora mosaics, Galla Placidia's starry mausoleum.
  • Medieval communes: Bologna's Archiginnasio, San Marino's Three Towers, Florence's Duomo, Uffizi, and Santa Croce, Pisa's Leaning Tower.
  • Modern exile: Elba – Napoleon's Villa dei Mulini and Villa San Martino.
